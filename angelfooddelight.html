Grandma Castle struggled with mental health issues but she loved her grand children more than anything in the world. She wanted us to have everything and would literally give us the shirt off of her back if she thought it'd make us happy. She was a struggle to be around the last decade or so but the Grandma Castle that I choose to remember was the one from my childhood who loved us and cared for us deeply.

Angel Food Delight

Remembering recipes wasn't easy but this recipe was one of the first to come to mind. It was definitely a staple in our house for a good stretch of the nineties. It was served in a large glass bowl, perhaps a trifle bowl. There was also an iteration that used Heath Bars as well. They were both very good.

Ingredients

  • 1 (16 oz) angel food cake, pinched or torn into very small pieces
  • 8 Butterfinger bars, crushed very fine
  • 1 stick butter, melted
  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 (16 oz) Cool Whip

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix melted butter, Cool Whip, and powdered sugar together until combined.
  2. Tear the angel food cake into very small pieces.
  3. Crush the Butterfinger candy very fine.
  4. In a serving dish, layer cake pieces first.
  5. Add a layer of the Cool Whip mixture.
  6. Add a layer of crushed candy.
  7. Repeat layers until ingredients are used, ending with a Cool Whip layer and candy on top.
  8. Chill before serving for best texture.

Recipe Summary

This typed version was interpreted from a handwritten note card. If your family version uses different candy bar sizes or a different pan size, adjust layers and sweetness to taste.
